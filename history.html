CCSF Coders Club History

Katherine Moloney

The Coders club was born under the co-founders: Katherine Moloney and Thomas “Chuck” Griffin. Their idea was to create an organization at the City College of San Francisco where students can share advice about programming, be social and get together to hack.


Chuck Griffin

The first meeting held on August 27th, 2012 at Science Hall, Room 037 at CCSF with 11 members. The Coders Google Group was established early on in order to maintain ongoing contact with club members and alumni.


Spring 2013 was first semester for the Coders club to be officially recognized by CCSF . Meeting were moved to Batmale 413, alongside the Linux Users Group (LUG), who we often shared resources, talks and advice. Katherine Moloney was the first president of the Coders club, followed by Thomas “Chuck” Griffin as the second president in Fall 2013.


Even though both co-founders are both employed full-time in the tech industry, they continually give back to Coders, providing insight and mentoring new leaders for programming careers in the San Francisco Bay Area.


Furthermore, Katherine established the Women-Who-Code club at CCSF, modeled after the Women-Who-Code-SF in order to support women interested in computer science at our community college.


The Coders club strive to motivate students to join the bustling tech community as well as participating in online open-source projects. Coding is fun but coding together is awesome!
